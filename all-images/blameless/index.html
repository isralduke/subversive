blameless

titus 1:7–8

“For the overseer must be blameless as one entrusted with God’s work, not arrogant, not prone to anger, not a drunkard, not violent, not greedy for gain. Instead he must be hospitable, devoted to what is good, sensible, upright, devout, and self-controlled.”
— titus 1:7–8
Read titus 1:7–8 »

In a social context that involves more than one person, there’s bound to be differences of opinion, different ideas on the definition of expected behavior. In this verse, Paul writes to Titus the qualifications of such an “overseer” for an assembly of believers. Being an “overseer” requires some explicit behavioral conditions.

As Paul specifically detailed the behavioral qualities an “overseer” should possess and should not possess, I thought it best to present these verses’ collective rendition as black-and-white, sans any grey. This verse belong belongs to a collection of verses in which there is a definite black-and-white binary set of instructions.

I couldn’t help but think of the famed justice scale for these contrasts. So, the “handle” is the word “blameless” while the weights are the qualities that Paul compares. You get extra points if you can tell why the “is” and “not” parenthetical containers face in different directions.
