exactly perfect

hebrews 1:3

“The Son is the radiance of his glory and the representation of his essence, and he sustains all things by his powerful word, and so when he had accomplished cleansing for sins, he sat down at the right hand of the Majesty on high.”
— hebrews 1:3
Read hebrews 1:3 »

I remember from an early university philosophy class that Plato held the idea to be better than the form. The idea was perfect, while the form was imperfect because it could never be as good as the idea from which it descended.

Paul, the Biblical writer, and John the Gospel writer, firmly held that it’s indeed possible for the form to be identical to the idea. Paul wrote in Colossians 1:15 and hebrews 1:3 that Jesus proves it to be true, for He is the exactly perfect image of God. C.S. Lewis, the late Cambridge professor and apologist, writes that to beget something is to bring about something identical to the begetter, that the begotten and the begetter are exactly the same.

These writers are claiming that Jesus is both the the grand idea and the grand form: that Jesus is God. They echo Jesus’ own earlier words in John 14:8–11 that He Himself and the Father are one and the same..
