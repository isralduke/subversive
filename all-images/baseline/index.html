Read luke 23:39–44 »

Belief is the only requirement.

Many folks will brag about their morally-superior attitude, opinions, or their morally-superior life. In this attitude they place themselves about some other person or group. Some churchgoers will tout their own self-sense of behavioral superiority saying things that connect their moral value to their behavior. “I believe in Christianity, I am a good person. I will go to heaven when I leave this life.”

Not only is the belief that ones’ behavior makes one destined for heaven incorrect by biblical standards and beside the point, but Jesus invalidates that opinion while He suffered on the cross. The story of the repentant thief demonstrates this.

During the report of the crucifixion in Luke, one of the two thieves crucified with Jesus recognized both Jesus' divinity and sinless existence. When the other, unrepentant thief demanded exoneration without justice, the repentant thief chided him for it. As the repentant thief knew his own guilt and Jesus' innocence, all he could do was ask Jesus for salvation. He never had a chance to do any good things which some religious folk use to hang their self-righteous crowns. Jesus granted him eternal life in paradise.
