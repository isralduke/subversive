think whatever

Philippians 4:8

“Finally, brothers and sisters, whatever is true, whatever is worthy of respect, whatever is just, whatever is pure, whatever is lovely, whatever is commendable, if something is excellent or praiseworthy, think about these things.”
— Philippians 4:8
Read Philippians 4:8 »

Paul’s advice to the Philippians to in his letter does not lend itself to a laissez faire approach to faith, instead of begins with the idea that self-control begins in disciplining your thoughts.

He definitely holds that controlling one’s thoughts to be a key factor in spiritual health. It’s not the first time that Paul has advocated self-control to his audience.

As this verse is undeniably something that Paul recommends to be good for us, I thought it best to use bright, warm, energetic colors. Note that the lowercase “i” suggests a happy person while the dot atop that lowercase “i” leads your eye into a thought balloon containing Paul’s recommendations.
