no condemnation

john 8:11

“She replied, ‘No one, Lord.’ And Jesus said, ‘I do not condemn you either. Go, and from now on do not sin any more.”
— john 8:11
Read john 8:11 »

This piece is inspired by an Ardy Warhol rendition of the same biblical passage. My decision to make this entry to the Subversive Series collection was inspired by Andy Warhol’s own item inspired from the same Bible verse. .

There’s a number of errors and injustices being committed in the name of Christ.

Yet, there is more to the example that Jesus sets in John 8:11. In this verse, a number of Pharisees, the religious uptight of Jesus’ day, bring a woman, an alleged adulteress, to Jesus so that they can test His response to their own verdict they’ve imposed upon her.

He asks the Pharisees first to examine themselves allowing any who are guiltless in their group to throw the first stone. The Pharisees leave the scene slowly, realizing that they have no room to impose enforcement because of their own guilt.

Jesus then asks the woman where her accusers have gone. She tells Him that they’ve gone away. He tells her that He doesn’t judge but that she should go and live an exemplary life, that she should “sin no more.”

There isn’t any textual evidence for whether the woman was guilty of the accusation. It doesn’t matter, for Jesus Himself knew that He would provide the relief for all people from <a href="https://net.bible.org/#!bible/Romans+6:23" target"_blank">the eternal consequence of all their sin .

There is no one who’s guiltless. There is no one who can correctly, justifiably cast judgement upon another human, whether they are living their life properly. This is Christ’s duty alone.
