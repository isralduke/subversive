inversion

isaiah 5:20

“Those who call evil good and good evil are as good as dead, who turn darkness into light and light into darkness, who turn bitter into sweet and sweet into bitter.”
— isaiah 5:20
Read isaiah 5:20 »

As we move through history humanity doubtless changes its mind about many things. Then, as each of us grows older we change our minds and tastes. We put away childish things and slowly assume the mantle of adulthood. This growth occurs too for all of history across the globe and throughout humanity’s tenure upon the Earth.

Yet, there are some things we should never change, as these are some of the metrics that we as a species use to mark our movements, to guard against transgressing each other. These things are our values, how we guide ourselves in our relations to each other, as individuals, and as nations.

When we transpose the extremes, we introduce confusion into our decision making process and we lose our footing. It’s even worse when we swap those extremes intentionally just so we can feel good about choosing the things we read aren’t any good for ourselves or our fellow people. This is the warning given by Isaiah 5:20.
